Chennakesava Swamy Temple

Sri Prasanna Chennakesava Swamy Temple, located in Ongole, Andhra Pradesh, is a significant Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, worshipped here as Chennakesava Swamy, meaning 'Beautiful Lord'. The temple showcases remarkable Dravidian architecture and has been a center of devotion for centuries.

Historical Background

Construction Period: The temple dates back to the 16th century, constructed during the reign of Rama Bhadhra Raju and Raghupathy Raju under the Vijayanagara Empire. Architectural Significance: The temple stands as a fine example of Dravidian architecture, reflecting the grandeur of the Vijayanagara period.

Management and Ownership:

The temple is managed by a trust or committee responsible for its upkeep and the conduct of rituals. Specific details about the current managing body are not readily available.

Festivals and Celebrations

Brahmotsavam: An annual festival attracting devotees from across the region, featuring elaborate rituals and cultural performances. Vaikuntha Ekadasi: Celebrated with special prayers and ceremonies, marking the significance of this auspicious day in the Vaishnavite tradition. Krishna Janmashtami: Commemorating the birth of Lord Krishna with devotional singing, dancing, and enactments of Krishna's life stories. Tholi Ekadasi: Observed with special pujas and a large gathering of devotees, reflecting the temple's vibrant festive atmosphere.
