About Ongole City

Geography & Administration

Ongole is the headquarters of Prakasam district. It is located on the southeastern coast of India, near the Bay of Bengal. The city is well connected by road and rail, with the NH-16 (Chennai-Kolkata highway) passing through it.

Historical Significance

Ongole has ancient roots, dating back to the Maurya and Satavahana dynasties. It was historically ruled by the Pallavas, Cholas, and Vijayanagara kings. The famous Chennakesava Swamy Temple, built during the Vijayanagara era, is a major historical site.

Ongole Cattle (Ongole Bulls)

The city is internationally famous for the Ongole breed of cattle. These bulls are known for their strength, resistance to diseases, and high milk yield. Ongole cattle are exported to various countries for breeding purposes.

Economy & Industries

Agriculture is a major occupation, with crops like paddy, tobacco, and pulses grown here. The city has granite industries, producing high-quality black galaxy granite, which is exported worldwide. Trading and business hubs flourish in the city due to its connectivity.

Education & Institutions

Ongole has reputed institutions like Rao Bahadur Y. Mahabaleshwarappa Engineering College, QIS Institute of Technology, and other schools and colleges. Many students from Ongole pursue higher education in engineering, medicine, and management.

Tourism & Attractions

Kothapatnam Beach – A serene beach near Ongole. Chennakesava Swamy Temple – A famous pilgrimage site. Vodarevu Beach – Another beautiful coastal attraction nearby. Bhavanasi Lake – A scenic spot for nature lovers.

Transport & Connectivity

Railway: Ongole Railway Station is an important station on the Chennai-Howrah railway line. Road: NH-16 passes through Ongole, making road transport convenient. Air: The nearest airport is Vijayawada International Airport, about 150 km away.
